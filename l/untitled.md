# Laws

Conway’s law - an organization tends to produce systems that are a copy of its communication structure\
Goodhart’s Law - when a measure becomes a target, it ceases to be a good measure.\
Shirky principle - institutions will try to preserve the problem to which they are the solution.\
Pareto Principle - roughly 80% of consequences come from 20% of the causes.\
Brooks’s Law - adding people to a late project makes it later\
Hofstadter’s Law - it always takes longer than you expect, even when you take into account Hofstadter’s Law.\
Gall’s Law - complex can evolve from simple, but complex from the start will not function\
Spolsky’s law - all non-trivial abstractions to some degree are leaky\
Hyrum’s Law - given enough users of an interface, all observed behavior becomes a contract\
Kernighan’s Law - since debugging is twice as hard as writing, cleverness while writing is something to be avoided\
Knuth’s law - premature optimization is the root of all evil\
Broken Window Theory - neglect accelerates bad code patterns faster than any other factor
